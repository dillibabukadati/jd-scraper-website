This Privacy Policy describes how JD Scraper Pro ("we", "us", or "our") handles your information when you use our Chrome extension. We are committed to protecting your privacy and being transparent about our data practices.

Key Points:
  • We do NOT collect, store, or transmit your personal data to our servers
  • All scraping happens locally in your browser
  • Google OAuth is used only for exporting to your personal Google Sheets
  • We do NOT have access to your Google account data

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Data You Scrape

The extension scrapes publicly available business information from JustDial.com including:

  • Business names
  • Business addresses
  • Phone numbers
  • Ratings and review counts
  • Business listing URLs

Important: This data is stored ONLY in your browser's local storage. We do not have access to this data and it never leaves your device unless you explicitly export it to your own Google Sheets.

1.2 Google OAuth Data

When you choose to export data to Google Sheets, we request the following permissions:

  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/spreadsheets - To create and edit spreadsheets in your Google account
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/drive.file - To create a folder and manage only the files created by this extension

These permissions are handled through Chrome's Identity API. We do NOT store your Google authentication tokens or have access to any other files in your Google Drive.

2. How We Use Your Information

2.1 Local Processing

All scraping and data processing occurs locally in your browser. The extension:

  • Extracts data from JustDial pages you visit
  • Stores scraped data in Chrome's local storage (on your device only)
  • Processes queue items sequentially
  • Formats data for CSV or Google Sheets export

2.2 Google Sheets Export

When you click "Save to Sheets", the extension:

  • Authenticates you with Google using OAuth 2.0
  • Creates a folder named "JustDial Scraper Data" in YOUR Google Drive
  • Creates a new spreadsheet with your scraped data
  • Writes data directly from your browser to your Google account

We do NOT:

  • Store your OAuth tokens on our servers
  • Access your other Google Drive files
  • Read or modify spreadsheets you didn't create with this extension
  • Transmit your data to any third-party servers

3. Data Storage and Retention

3.1 Local Storage

Scraped data is stored in Chrome's local storage on your device. This data persists until you:

  • Click "Clear Data" in the extension
  • Clear your browser data
  • Uninstall the extension

3.2 Google Drive Storage

When you export to Google Sheets, the data is stored in YOUR Google Drive account. You have full control over these files and can delete them at any time. We do not retain copies of this data.

4. Third-Party Services

4.1 JustDial.com

The extension scrapes publicly available data from JustDial.com. We are not affiliated with JustDial and do not control their privacy practices. Please review JustDial's privacy policy for information about data they collect.

4.2 Google APIs

We use Google Sheets API and Google Drive API to enable the export feature. These APIs are subject to Google's privacy policies. Authentication is handled through Chrome's Identity API, and tokens are managed by Chrome, not by us.

5. Data Security

We implement the following security measures:

  • All data processing happens locally in your browser
  • OAuth tokens are managed securely by Chrome's Identity API
  • We use HTTPS for all API communications
  • We request minimal permissions necessary for functionality
  • We use the restrictive drive.file scope (not full Drive access)

6. Your Rights and Choices

6.1 Data Control

You have complete control over your data:

  • Clear Local Data: Click "Clear Data" button in the extension
  • Revoke Google Access: Visit Google Account Permissions
  • Delete Exported Files: Delete spreadsheets and folders from your Google Drive
  • Uninstall Extension: Remove the extension from Chrome to delete all local data

6.2 No Tracking

We do NOT use:

  • Analytics or tracking tools
  • Cookies (except those required by Chrome and Google APIs)
  • Third-party advertising
  • User behavior monitoring
